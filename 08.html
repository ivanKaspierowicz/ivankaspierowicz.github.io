University projects

The most outstanding projects through my degree

2018 - 2022

This section showcases some of the most important projects I have participated in during my time at university. To provide a clearer understanding of the range of assignments and the workload involved, you can visit the following link. "Ing. De Sonido - Plan 2007" (spanish only).

Auditorium design (2019): A new concert hall was designed on the site of the existing Teatro Auditorium in Buenos Aires. The main external noise sources identified were pedestrian movement and heavy traffic. The building program included a lobby, ticket office, cafeteria/bar, dressing and practice rooms for performers, restrooms, staff offices, machine rooms, and the main hall. Within the hall, 960 seats were arranged across two levels in accordance with Buenos Aires City building codes. The selected materials resulted in a reverberation time (RT) of 1.97 s at mid frequencies. A triangular reflector panel was designed above the front audience area to improve the initial time delay gap, maintaining it between 15 ms and 35 ms. Acoustic simulations indicated that the final design provided an SPL variation of less than 4 dB, an EDT of 1.63 s at 1 kHz, and an IAGG of 0.25 s at 1 kHz, ensuring balanced sound coverage and high acoustic quality for the audience.

APC is a software tool developed for room impulse response (RIR) processing, designed to calculate key acoustical parameters such as EDT, T20, T30, C50, C80, IACC, and IACC-early, in accordance with ISO 3382. In addition, the software computes the transition time (Tt), enabling the calculation of EDTt, CTt, and IACCt, which provide valuable insight into early sound field behavior.

Calculations can be performed for octave or third-octave bands, following IEC 61260 filter specifications. The program also supports multiple RIR processing, allowing users to analyze either averaged or individual measurements. The developed software was compared with commercial tools such as EASERA, Aurora, and AARAE, demonstrating good agreement and consistent results. The code was written in Python and is available online for public access.

Control room design (2021): The project involved the design of an anechoic control room for a recording studio with a capacity for eight people, in compliance with DISPO regulations of CABA, Argentina. Five different monitoring systems were implemented: two for stereo productions, one for binaural audio, and one for 3D audio productions. The design also included detailed acoustic treatment and soundproofing calculations. The measured reverberation times were within the tolerances established by the AES. To support the design, several analytical tools and methods were employed. Boundary Element Method (BEM) simulations were used to analyze room modes below the Schroeder frequency, while absorption and diffusion simulations were conducted to achieve an optimal reverberation time (RT) across all frequency bands. Additionally, transmission loss calculations were performed to ensure compliance with Law No. 1540. The study also included an analysis of multichannel monitoring configurations and a review of commercially available loudspeakers, as well as the ventilation requirements necessary to maintain low CO₂ levels within the control room.
