University projects

The most outstandings projects through my degree

2018 - 2022

This section reveals some of the most important projects that i have been made through my journey in the university. For context, you can see the wide range of assigments and the workload in the following link "Ing. De Sonido - Plan 2007" (spanish only).

Auditorium design (2019): A concert hall was designed at the site of the existing auditorium, Teatro Auditorium in Buenos Aires. Possible surrounding noise sources are people movement and heavy traffic. The building consists of a lobby, ticket office, cafeteria/bar, dressing rooms and practice rooms for performers, restroom, staff office, machine rooms as well as main hall space. In the hall, 960 seats were arranged in two levels according to Buenos Aires city’s building code. The materials realized the reverberation time of 1.97 s at middle frequencies. The triangular reflector panel was designed above the frontal audience to improve the initial time delay gap between 15 ms and 35 ms. The concert hall design through acoustic simulation software provides to the audience an SPL variation less than 4 dB, an EDT of 1.63 s at 1 kHz, and an IAGG of 0.25 s at 1 kHz.

RIR software (2021): APC is a software developed for the room impulse response processing for calculate the acoustical parameters EDT, T20, T30, C50, C80, IACC, and IACC-early, according to ISO-3382. In addition, transition time (Tt) is calculated, allowing to calculate EDTt, CTt, and IACCt, and giving an approach of early sound field behavior. Calculations can be made for octave or third-octave bands, according to IEC-61260 filters specifications. Also, multiple RIRs can be processed, allowing the user to analyze the averaged or the individual measurements. The software developed is compared with commercial software EASERA, Aurora, and AARAE, showing good and consistent results. The code is written in Python language and published online.

Control room design (2021): Design of a ambechoic control room for a recording studio, with 8 people capacity according to the DISPO regulations of CABA, Argentina. Five different monitoring systems were installed, two for stereo productions, one for binaural audio productions, and one for 3D audio productions. Acoustic treatment and soundproofing calculations are also included. The obtained reverberation time were within the tolerances established by the AES. Some tools used to provide the design of the control room are the BEM (Boundary element method) simulations to study the modes below the Schroeder frequency, the simulation of the absorption and diffusion to provide an optimal RT all across the frequency bands, and the trasmission loss calculation in order to satisfy the law N°1540. Furthermore, the analysis of multichannel monitoring and the speakers often in the market, and the calculation of the ventilation required to keep low levels of C02 in the control room.
