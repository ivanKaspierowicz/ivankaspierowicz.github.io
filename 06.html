Auditorium design

1Q - 2019

Abstract: A concert hall was designed at the site of the existing auditorium, Teatro Auditorium in Buenos Aires. Possible surrounding noise sources are people movement and heavy traffic. The building consists of a lobby, ticket office, cafeteria/bar, dressing rooms and practice rooms for performers, restroom, staff office, machine rooms as well as main hall space. In the hall, 960 seats were arranged in two levels according to Buenos Aires city’s building code. The materials realized the reverberation time of 1.97 s at middle frequencies. The triangular reflector panel was designed above the frontal audience to improve the initial time delay gap between 15 ms and 35 ms. The concert hall design through acoustic simulation software provides to the audience an SPL variation less than 4 dB, an EDT of 1.63 s at 1 kHz, and an IAGG of 0.25 s at 1 kHz.

The San Martin concert hall a has capacity for 960 spectators and 12800 m3 as a result a relation volume per spectator of 13.3 m3 and has ten exit doors 1.5 m each. The main floor has a capacity for 680 spectators and it has two special sites for people with disabilities. In addition, the maximum longitudinal length is 42.48 meters, the stage has 219 m2, and has a separate elevated choir balcony. The balcony has space for 240 spectators, two exit doors 1.5 meters each, and two main corridors of 1.2 meters. All the design complies with the Buenos Aires city's building code, ten doors (150 meters in total), all the corridors has 1.2 m as a minimum, the distance between seats is 0.5 m and seat dimensions are 0.5 between arms, a back-rest of 0.5m height and a depth of 0.45 m.

To acquire the values for all acoustic parameters it was used the same receiver points(seats) were into a room model from the entire auditorium. Unfortunately, the software cannot import reflector design and the stairs on the balcony. Using a computer software trougth ray tracing method with reflection order 5 and 30000 rays for one speaker, ITDG values, G and SPL variations for nine seats (six from the main floor and three from the balcony) are obtained. Then a binaural response for each seat is exported to calculate the IACC and EDT parameters. Finally, with the binaural response in a seat a simulation of how a Cello Sound in the auditorium is realized using the convolution technique.
