Control room design

1Q - 2021

Abstract: This document describes the design of a small ambechoic control room for a recording studio, for 8 people according to the DISPO regulations of CABA, Argentina. Five different monitoring systems were installed, two for stereo productions, one for binaural audio productions, and one for 3D audio productions. Acoustic treatment and soundproofing calculations are also included. The reverberation time obtained in the different frequency bands were within the tolerances established by the AES.



The ambechoic spaces refer to the “ambient anechoic rooms”, conceptually these ambients provide a uniform acoustical environment in rooms which utilize surround systems. This concept was developed by Petrović and Dadović [3] to apply in small rooms (less than 80 m3). The basic initial design conditions defined by them are homogenous diffusion on all surfaces above approximately 1 kHz, except the floor; and maximum possible absorption in frequencies below 250 Hz, homogenous on all surfaces except the floor.


Some tools used to provide the design of the control room are the BEM (Boundary element method) simulations to study the modes below the Schreder frequency, the simulation of the absorption and diffusion to provide an optimal RT all across the frequency bands, and the trasmission loss calculation in order to satisfy the law N°1540. Furthermore, the analysis of multichannel monitoring and the speakers often in the market, and the calculation of the ventilation required to keep low levels of C02 in the control room.
