Microphone measurements

2Q - 2019

Abstract: This measurement report the behavior of three microphones through measurement by transfer function method taking as reference the Earthworks M-50 with flat response, the results obtained were that in first place the RE-320 microphone is limited between (60 Hz - 12 kHz), with +6 dB boost at high frequencies. Secondly, the HSR3.2 whose frequency response is limited between (32 Hz; 16 kHz), with two resonances in low frequencies and a boost in high frequencies. Finally, the frequency response of the Samsom Q6 is reduced between (60 Hz; 16 kHz), with a lot of attenuation in low frequencies, a resonance in 200 Hz, and a boost in high frequencies. The measured data are validated and compared with the specifications provided by the manufacturers, these being similar, except in the case of the EV-RE320 which will be discussed throughout the report.




Three types of measurements are used: Frequency response (front-back comparison) for every microphone; directivity for the EV-RE320 and the HSA-2 in bidirectional pattern; and proximity effect for the EV-RE320 and the HSA-2 in cardioid pattern. In all the measurements a transfer function method is used with a pseudo-aleatory pink noise audio input in order to realize the coherence analysis.
